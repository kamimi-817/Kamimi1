Susanoo-no-Mikoto

Hello! Here, we introduce the deity enshrined, Susanoo-no-Mikoto.

Who is Susanoo-no-Mikoto?

Susanoo-no-Mikoto is a kami from Japanese mythology who governs storms and the sea. He is the younger brother of Amaterasu Omikami (the Sun Goddess) and Tsukuyomi-no-Mikoto (the Moon God), and the child of Izanagi-no-Mikoto and Izanami-no-Mikoto.

Examples of Prayers

When praying to Susanoo-no-Mikoto, the following requests are appropriate:

  • Protection and Safety: To protect oneself from calamities and wish for daily safety.
  • Health: To recover from illness or to maintain good health.
  • Success in Love: To deepen bonds with a loved one and build a happy relationship.
  • Success in Work and Study: To achieve goals, excel in studies, and pave the way for the future.

    • What is His Personality Like?

    Susanoo-no-Mikoto is a very powerful and brave deity, but his personality is complex. He is emotionally rich and can act impulsively, but behind his actions lies great affection and a sense of justice. Although he sometimes causes trouble among other gods and people, he is ultimately respected for his strength and sincerity, as he solves many problems and protects people.

    Main Episodes

    Amaterasu’s Hiding in the Rock Cave: Susanoo-no-Mikoto’s unruly behavior caused Amaterasu Omikami to become so enraged that she hid in the Heavenly Rock Cave. His rampage in the heavenly realm and destruction of Amaterasu’s shrine plunged the world into darkness. Tajikarao-no-Mikoto, working with other gods, opened the cave and restored light to the world.

    Defeating the Eight-Headed Serpent (Yamata-no-Orochi): One of Susanoo-no-Mikoto’s most famous stories is his battle with the eight-headed serpent. This giant snake was tormenting villagers, but Susanoo-no-Mikoto defeated it. During this battle, he discovered the legendary sword, Kusanagi-no-Tsurugi. Originally known as the "Ame-no-Murakumo-no-Tsurugi," it became known as Kusanagi-no-Tsurugi and later became one of Japan’s Three Sacred Treasures. The Kusanagi-no-Tsurugi has been passed down to the emperors of Japan as a symbol of rightful rule.

    What Role Does He Play?

    Susanoo-no-Mikoto plays a crucial role in calming storms and controlling the forces of nature. He is believed to give courage to people facing difficulties and to have the power to protect them from disasters.

    By learning about Susanoo-no-Mikoto’s stories and role, you can experience his power and charm. When facing difficulties or taking new steps, praying to Susanoo-no-Mikoto can bring you his strong divine virtues. Please consider visiting and praying to Susanoo-no-Mikoto yourself.
