高龗神

Takao-kami-no-Kami

Hello! In this page, we will introduce Takao-kami-no-Kami, the deity enshrined at Kifune Shinto Shrine.

Who is Takao-kami-no-Kami?

Takao-kami-no-Kami is an important kami who governs water and is known as a dragon deity with the power to bring and stop rain. He also appears in Japan’s ancient texts such as "Kojiki" and "Nihon Shoki." Takao-kami-no-Kami was born when Kagutsuchi, the kami of fire, was cut down. To calm the disasters caused by fire, Takao-kami-no-Kami emerged as a water kami.

What are the blessings of Takao-kami-no-Kami?

  • The power to bring and stop rain: Takao-kami-no-Kami is worshiped as a dragon kami who brings rain and stops it when necessary. He has been believed to bless those who pray for agriculture and natural blessings, particularly in relation to rain that affects crops.
  • The power to improve fortune: Takao-kami-no-Kami brings positive changes to life and improves fortune. He is recommended for those wishing to embark on a new journey or to move forward on a better path.
  • The power to grant various wishes (fulfillment of desires): It is said that Takao-kami-no-Kami can grant any wish, whether for career success, health, or family happiness, if you pray with sincerity.
  • The power to form good relationships: Kifune Shinto Shrine is also known for its role in matchmaking, and one of the reasons lies in Takao-kami-no-Kami. Water sources have long been seen as places where people meet and interact, and they have been believed to gather connections, not just in love but also in friendships and business relationships. There’s even a tale from the Heian period of the poetess Izumi Shikibu visiting Kifune Shrine and restoring her husband’s love after struggling with marital discord.

What role does Takao-kami-no-Kami play?

Takao-kami-no-Kami is a kami who supports people's lives by bringing natural blessings through the power to bring and stop rain. As the deity who governs water, Takao-kami-no-Kami adjusts the amount of rain to ensure the right balance, thus protecting our lives by providing the necessary water for agriculture and the environment.

In addition to rain, Takao-kami-no-Kami is said to have the power to improve fortune and form connections, affecting various aspects of our lives.

Daily Gratitude

We receive the blessings of water and nature daily as if it were only natural, but it is thanks to the unseen powers of kami like Takao-kami-no-Kami. When visiting a shrine, do not forget to express your gratitude in addition to making your wishes.

Main Episodes

The Birth of Takao-kami-no-Kami: Takao-kami-no-Kami is the water kami born when Kagutsuchi, the kami of fire, was cut down. According to Japanese mythology, when Izanagi-no-Mikoto cut Kagutsuchi, various kami were born from the blood. One of these was Takao-kami-no-Kami, who was born to suppress the power of fire by becoming a water kami.

Relation to Kuraokami: The Takao-kami-no-Kami enshrined at Kifune Shinto Shrine is also known as Kuraokami. According to the shrine records, "though the names are different, they are the same kami." One theory suggests that Takao-kami-no-Kami is a "dragon deity of the mountaintop," while Kuraokami is the "dragon deity of the valley depths." Both are water kami, and as water is the source of all life, they play an important role in maintaining the balance of nature as "kami of the water source."

While we may take the blessings of water and nature for granted in our daily lives, it is kami like Takao-kami-no-Kami who protect us. When visiting a shrine, remember not only to make wishes but also to express your daily gratitude.
